# Germinal Theory
## A Framework for Cultivating Systems Through Their Nature
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Foundational Document — Expandable Core

## What This Is
Germinal Theory is a framework for cultivating systems through their nature. It is not a method—it is a **stance**, a way of being in relation to problems, systems, and reality itself.

## The Core Insight
> Every problem has a nature—a shape, a tendency, a direction it wants to go. Your role is to observe that nature, create the conditions for it to express itself, constrain it so it doesn't collapse, and harness its emergent power. You do not force. You cultivate.
## The Five Principles
1. **Observe** — The nature is already there. Your job is to see it, not to create it.
2. **Cultivate** — You do not force growth. You prepare the conditions.
3. **Constrain** — You do not control. You set boundaries.
4. **Harness** — You do not push. You guide emergent energy.
5. **Trust** — The nature is already there. You do not need to invent it.
## The Tagline
> *Find the nature. Work with it. Get out of the way.*
