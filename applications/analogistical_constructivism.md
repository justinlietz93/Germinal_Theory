# Germinal Theory in Analogistical Constructivism
## The Germinal Recognition
Understanding is built through analogic mapping and logistical organization. The prior structure is already there. Your job is to map it, not to create it.
## Analogistical Constructivism as Germinal
Analogistical Constructivism is a framework for learning and cognition. It shares the same posture as Germinal Theory.
### Observe
**Notice the prior structure before you impose your own.**
The learner has a prior structure—a tendency, a shape, a direction they want to go. Your job is to see it, not to force it.
### Cultivate
**Provide analogies, context, and time. Let understanding grow.**
The understanding is already latent in the prior structure. Your role is to create the conditions for it to emerge.
### Constrain
**Set constraints on attention, energy, and time. Let insight find its own path.**
You do not control the learning process. You set the boundaries—the container for understanding to grow.
### Harness
**Follow the curiosity. Let the question lead you.**
The learner has their own momentum. Your role is to work with that momentum—to guide it, channel it, harness it.
### Trust
**Trust that understanding will grow.**
Trust the process. Trust the learner. Trust the prior structure.
## The Core Insight
> Meaning is built analogically; mastery is built logistically.
## The Takeaway
> The prior structure is already there. Your job is to map it, not to create it.
> *Find the nature. Work with it. Get out of the way.*
