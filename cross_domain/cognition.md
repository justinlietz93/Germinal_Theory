# Germinal Theory in Cognition
## The Germinal Recognition
The prior structure is already there. Your job is to map it, not to create it.
## Core Principles in Cognition
### Observe
**Notice the structure before you impose your own.**
Before you learn, before you teach, before you intervene—observe. The learner has a prior structure. The understanding is latent. Your job is to make it visible.
### Cultivate
**Provide analogies, context, and time. Let understanding grow.**
The understanding is already present in the prior structure. Your role is to create the conditions for it to emerge.
### Constrain
**Set constraints on attention, energy, and time. Let insight find its own path.**
You do not control the learning process. You set the boundaries—the container—and let the learner find their own path.
### Harness
**Follow the curiosity. Let the question lead you.**
The learner has their own momentum. Your role is to work with that momentum—to guide it, channel it, harness it.
### Trust
**The understanding is already latent. You just have to uncover it.**
Trust the process. Trust the learner. Trust the prior structure.
## Anti-Patterns in Cognition
| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Forcing** | Imposing a solution before understanding the learner's prior structure. |
| **Rote Memorization** | Forcing isolated facts before a unifying architecture forms. |
| **Premature Atomization** | Starting too low-level and demanding token retention before the learner can grasp the larger structure. |
| **Skepticism as Identity** | Doubt, mistrust, fear of error. Produces paralysis and calls it rigor. |
## Example: Analogistical Constructivism
- **Observe:** The learner's prior structure.
- **Cultivate:** Provide analogies and context.
- **Constrain:** Set boundaries on attention and energy.
- **Harness:** Follow the learner's curiosity.
- **Trust:** Trust that understanding will grow.
## The Takeaway
> The prior structure is already there. Your job is to map it, not to create it.
> *Find the nature. Work with it. Get out of the way.*
