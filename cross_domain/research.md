# Germinal Theory in Research
## The Germinal Recognition
Curiosity is the drive. Verification is what curiosity does when it has not finished asking.
## Core Principles in Research
### Observe
**See the nature of the problem before you design the study.**
Before you hypothesize, before you experiment, before you publish—observe. The question has a shape. The answer is latent. Your job is to make it visible.
### Cultivate
**Create conditions for discovery to emerge.**
The discovery is already present in the question. Your role is to create the conditions for it to emerge—time, resources, curiosity, safety.
### Constrain
**Set boundaries—no fear of error, no gatekeeping.**
You do not control the outcome. You set the boundaries—the container for curiosity to do its work.
### Harness
**Work with curiosity, not against it.**
Curiosity has its own momentum. Your role is to work with that momentum—to guide it, channel it, harness it.
### Trust
**Trust that curiosity will verify itself.**
Trust the process. Trust the researcher. Trust the drive to know.
## Anti-Patterns in Research
| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Skepticism as Identity** | Doubt, mistrust, fear of error. Produces paralysis and calls it rigor. |
| **Gatekeeping** | Peer review that punishes novelty. Rewards the least objectionable idea, not the best. |
| **Premature Certainty** | Projecting certainty before discovery is complete. Suppresses curiosity. |
| **Fear of Exploration** | Treating exploratory research as fraud-adjacent. Suppresses discovery. |
## Example: "Attack This" Publishing
- **Observe:** The problem—peer review punishes novelty.
- **Cultivate:** Publish openly with all data, code, and negative controls.
- **Constrain:** Set the boundary—"Attack This."
- **Harness:** Let the community verify.
- **Trust:** Trust that curiosity will find the truth.
## The Takeaway
> Curiosity asks "what is true here?" Skepticism asks "what is wrong here?" The first question does not terminate. The second terminates at a list of objections.
> *Find the nature. Work with it. Get out of the way.*
