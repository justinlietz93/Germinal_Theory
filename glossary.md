# Germinal Theory — Glossary
## Core Terms
### Germ
The foundational, unchanging structure beneath the chaos. The nature of the system. The essential core.
### Germinal
Foundational, originating, generative. A seed from which something grows.
### Cultivation
Creating the conditions for growth. Preparing the soil, providing light and water, removing obstacles. Not forcing—tending.
### Constraint
A boundary that prevents collapse. Not control—a container that allows self-organization.
### Harnessing
Working with emergent energy. Guiding, channeling, directing—not forcing.
### Observation
Seeing the nature of the problem before acting. Not intervening—perceiving.
### Trust
Believing that the system will grow into its own shape. Letting go of the need to control.
### Emergence
The system's natural tendency to self-organize. What happens when conditions are right.
### Overbuilt
Making parts so robust, so forgiving, and so well-designed that they survive conditions far beyond their nominal requirements. Good complexity.
### Overengineered
Adding complexity to solve a problem that does not exist, or introducing new failure modes. Bad complexity.
### Soma
The temporary, changing expression of the germ. The body, the implementation, the transient form.
## Related Terms
### Analogistical
A fused precision construct combining analogic mapping and logistical organization.
### Platonic Form
The essential structure beneath the variations. What every child draws when they draw a "car."
### Pre-Foundational Structure
The underlying architecture that connects and drives everything. The structure beneath the words.
### Stance
A way of being in relation to problems, systems, and reality itself. Not a method—a posture.
## Anti-Patterns
### Forcing
Imposing your will before understanding the nature. Produces resistance and brittleness.
### Controlling
Micromanaging every variable. Produces fragility and cascading failure.
### Engineering (as commonly understood)
Designing outcomes instead of cultivating conditions. Produces systems that cannot adapt.
### Skepticism as Identity
Doubt, mistrust, fear of error. Produces paralysis and calls it rigor.
### Brittle Foundations
Building on a weak root and then adding support structures. The cracks propagate upward.
